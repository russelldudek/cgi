Disposition key

Permit

Bounded pattern, approved controls, acceptance evidence, telemetry and named ownership.

Conditional

Direction is sound; owned, measurable conditions must be met before release or capability enablement.

Hold

Outcome, boundary, evidence, visibility or ownership is too weak for responsible commitment.

Review sequence

Define the load. Outcome, behavior, scale, cost and consequence.
Select the bounded pattern. Shared services and legitimate variation.
Bind controls. Identity, data, security, authority, escalation and rollback.
Specify evidence. Acceptance, evaluation, red-team and regression before sunk-cost pressure.
Instrument operation. Quality, cost, drift, incidents, recovery and adoption.
Transfer ownership. Technical, business, risk and operating owners plus evolution cadence.

Exception discipline

Record the standard varied, reason, risk acceptance, compensating control, telemetry, owner, expiry and reassessment trigger. Repeated exceptions may signal a missing platform service or an unrealistic standard.

Promotion rule

A solution becomes a reference pattern only after repeatable implementation and production evidence show leverage without hiding domain-specific risk.
