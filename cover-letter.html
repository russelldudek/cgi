Dear CGI Hiring Team,

Your GenAI Ops Solution Architect role is not simply asking for someone who can diagram RAG and agentic systems. It is asking for an architecture authority who can make enterprise AI reusable: shared services that accelerate onboarding, governance that survives production pressure, evaluation that produces evidence, observability that reveals real behavior, and standards that engineering teams can actually own.

That mandate fits the through-line of my career. At Compunetics, I led engineering and operations in high-reliability technical environments where standards, traceability, quality controls and cross-functional decisions determined whether complex programs held. At Amazon, I built visible operating mechanisms in a 24/7 network supporting approximately five million second-day orders annually. Today at Vape-Jet, I connect production, support, quality, engineering, ERP, telemetry and AI-assisted workflows across a software-enabled machine fleet. Through DudeWorth, I directly design agentic and retrieval workflow patterns with structured context, human authority, evaluation, escalation and governance rails.

I also want to address the obvious question directly. I have not spent ten years under a formal enterprise-architecture title, and I would not claim three years owning the exact centralized GenAIOps platform described in your posting. What I bring is the adjacent evidence that matters when an architecture must become an operating reality: the ability to translate business consequences into technical boundaries, establish reusable mechanisms, connect evidence to decisions, and earn adoption across engineering, operations, risk and executive stakeholders.

My independent candidate vision at russelldudek.github.io/cgi/ makes that thinking visible through an Enterprise AI Building Code, an interactive Architecture Permit Review and a practical 90-day entry plan. The central idea is simple: enterprise AI scales when every use case enters through a load-bearing architecture—one that binds pattern, controls, evaluation, observability and ownership before production pressure exposes the gaps.

I would welcome a conversation about CGI’s current GenAIOps foundation, the client environment behind this role, and where architecture exceptions or fragmented ownership are creating the most drag today.

Carpe diem,
