Entry principle

Read the load before rewriting the structure. Reduce uncertainty, expose architecture friction and prove one reusable decision mechanism before declaring a new platform direction.

Days 1-15 · Orient

Establish the mandate and authority map.

Work

  • Interview business, engineering, platform, security, risk and operations leaders.
  • Review current architecture, roadmap, use-case portfolio and governance forums.
  • Map cloud, data, identity, integration and regulatory boundaries.

Evidence produced

Mandate statement; decision-rights map; source inventory; assumptions and open-questions ledger.

Gate: agree what this architect may decide, recommend and escalate.
Days 16-30 · Diagnose

Trace the real GenAIOps load paths.

Work

  • Follow representative RAG, agentic and document-intelligence use cases end to end.
  • Sample evaluation, telemetry, incident, exception and ownership records.
  • Locate repeat rework, waiting and late control discovery.

Evidence produced

Current-state map; exception taxonomy; architecture-friction baseline; pilot candidate and risk register.

Gate: choose a consequential but bounded pilot with visible ownership.
Days 31-60 · Codify + prove

Draft and test the minimum viable Building Code.

Work

  • Define intake, reference-pattern, control, evaluation, observability and authority requirements.
  • Run an Architecture Permit review with the pilot team.
  • Capture conditions, exceptions, decision latency, rework and evidence gaps.

Evidence produced

Permit v1; pattern-catalog outline; decision-record template; pilot disposition; evidence baseline.

Gate: no false green - permit, condition or hold based on evidence.
Days 61-90 · Refine + institutionalize

Turn pilot learning into reusable capability.

Work

  • Separate domain-specific needs from shared platform services.
  • Prioritize retrieval, agent gateway, evaluation, telemetry, identity, data and governance enhancements.
  • Establish review cadence, enablement, runbooks and ownership after go-live.

Evidence produced

Building Code v2; shared-service backlog; exception policy; scorecard; six-month roadmap and enablement plan.

Gate: transfer an owned operating mechanism, not a slide-deck standard.

Scorecard established by day 90

FlowPermit lead time, waiting by boundary and late-discovery rework.
ReusePattern adoption, shared-service use, exception rate and exception age.
EvidenceEvaluation coverage, trace completeness, incident and recovery visibility.
OwnershipNamed decision rights, unresolved handoffs and transfer progress.

Entry-plan guardrails

No platform rewrite before current-state evidence. No standard without a known user and exception path. No governance that records approvals but cannot observe production behavior. No pilot called successful without measured acceptance, named ownership and an explicit reuse decision.
