## Official identity and provenance

`assets/brand/cgi-logo.svg` is the unmodified `CGI_logo_color_rgb.svg` from CGI's official logo package. It is displayed proportionally and without recoloring, crop, animation, effects or integration into a candidate-created mark. The mark is used only for nominative identification of the intended employer and is paired with an immediate independent-candidate qualifier.

CGI's guidance identifies CGI Red as Pantone 186C / RGB 227, 25, 55 and states that the logo is unalterable. The campaign preserves those requirements.

## Typography evidence and implementation

CGI's public careers CSS declares Poppins and Source Sans Pro. No proprietary or remotely served font binaries were copied or committed. The campaign uses broadly available structural substitutes—Trebuchet MS / Avenir Next / Arial for geometric heading behavior and Segoe UI / Source Sans Pro / Arial for readable body copy—while reproducing the observed hierarchy, weight, tracking and density. The substitutes are not described as official CGI fonts.

## Photography and imagery decision

No company photography is used. The mandate is better explained through an original architecture-review environment, and avoiding client/facility imagery prevents any implication that a pictured system or result belongs to Russell.

## Brand-to-role translation

- **Company recognition:** one clear official CGI lockup above the fold, CGI Red, restrained editorial typography and precise rule/grid behavior.
- **Role metaphor:** an enterprise architecture inspection rather than a dashboard or generic network.
- **Signature hero:** an eight-layer Architecture Load Test linking outcome, pattern, data, identity, evaluation, observability, human authority and production ownership.
- **Role-derived motion:** one bounded test sequence runs after load or scenario selection; layers engage in causal order, gaps remain attached to the affected layer, and the disposition resolves only after the entire load path is inspected.
- **Meaningful interaction:** keyboard-operable use-case selection changes the failed controls, architecture evidence, next action and Permit / Conditional / Hold disposition.
- **Reduced motion:** all eight layers and the final decision resolve immediately with no loss of information or control.

The original visual system is not a recreation of a CGI product or internal interface.

## Document continuity

The résumé, cover letter, interview brief, entry plan and Architecture Permit carry a restrained version of the same system: official logo, CGI Red rule, precise section notation, architectural language and decision-state grammar. They remain candidate-owned, ATS-readable and do not imitate corporate letterhead.

## Independent-candidate distinction

The campaign states that it is independent candidate work and not sponsored or endorsed by CGI. Company identity answers who the work is for; the thesis, load test and permit mechanism communicate Russell Dudek's point of view.
